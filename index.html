About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in theoretical nuclear physics at Vanderbilt University, working under the supervision of Dr. A. S. Umar. My research sits at the intersection of many-body quantum theory and high-performance computing, developing and applying advanced numerical methods to solve fundamental problems in nuclear structure and reactions.

My work spans time-dependent density functional theory, relativistic quantum mechanics, and large-scale scientific software development in Fortran, C/C++, and Python. I am passionate about using computational tools to connect theoretical models with experimental data and push the boundaries of what is calculable in nuclear physics.

Research

My research develops and advances nuclear many-body models using many-body quantum theory, time-dependent density functional theory, electromagnetic theory, and relativistic quantum field theory.

Muon-Induced Fission

Studying muon-induced fission of actinide nuclei by solving the time-dependent Dirac equation, probing the underlying dynamics of nuclear fission through a novel electromagnetic mechanism.

Relativistic Quantum Mechanics on 3D Grids

Numerical solution of the 4-component Dirac equation with electromagnetic interactions on 3D Cartesian grids, enabling first-principles calculations of muonic atom dynamics.

Nucleon Transfer in Fusion

Investigating nucleon transfer mechanisms and the role of isospin composition in low-energy nuclear fusion reactions using time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory.

Pairing Dynamics & BCS Theory

Implementing time-dependent BCS pairing dynamics within the Cb-TDHFB framework to capture superfluidity effects in nuclear collisions.

Superheavy Element Synthesis

Studying quasifission dynamics and the determination of fission and quasifission fragment properties relevant to the synthesis of superheavy elements.

Software

I design and implement high-performance numerical software for nuclear physics, connecting theoretical models with experimental data.

muon_fission_v2

Muon-Induced Fission Simulator

Fortran 90 code solving the time-dependent Dirac equation for muonic atoms in fissioning actinide nuclei. Features 4-component spinor evolution on 3D grids with FFT-based Coulomb solver and OpenMP parallelization.

Fortran 90 OpenMP FFTW3
GitHub (coming soon)

TDHFBCS_v2

Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-BCS

Ground-up modernization of a legacy nuclear physics production code. Modular Fortran 90 architecture with section-based input system, checkpoint/restart capability, and automated build system.

Fortran 90 OpenMP LAPACK
GitHub (coming soon)

tdhf-3d

Legacy TDHF-BCS Maintenance

Ongoing maintenance and extension of the production VU-TDHF3D code used in published nuclear fusion and fission research.

Fortran MPI HPC
