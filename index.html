About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in theoretical nuclear physics at Vanderbilt University, working under the supervision of Dr. A. S. Umar. My research sits at the intersection of many-body quantum theory and high-performance computing, developing and applying advanced numerical methods to solve fundamental problems in nuclear structure and reactions. My current focus is on the fission of actinide nuclei, while also investigating quasifission dynamics and fusion reactions relevant to superheavy element production.

My work spans relativistic quantum mechanics, large-scale scientific software development in Fortran, C/C++, and Python, and time-dependent density functional theory. Our research group utilizes computational tools to connect theoretical models with experimental data and push the frontier of what is calculable in nuclear physics. Beyond my thesis work, I have a strong interest in quantum field theory, in particular, quantum chromodynamics and lattice methods, which I am actively exploring through independent projects.

Research

My research develops and advances nuclear many-body models using many-body quantum theory, time-dependent density functional theory, electromagnetic theory, and relativistic quantum field theory.

Muon-Induced Fission

Studying muon-induced fission of actinide nuclei by solving the time-dependent Dirac equation, probing the underlying dynamics of nuclear fission through a novel electromagnetic mechanism.

Relativistic Quantum Mechanics on 3D Grids

Numerical solution of the 4-component Dirac equation with electromagnetic interactions on 3D Cartesian grids, enabling first-principles calculations of muonic atom dynamics.

Nucleon Transfer in Fusion

Investigating nucleon transfer mechanisms and the role of isospin composition in low-energy nuclear fusion reactions using time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory.

Pairing Dynamics & BCS Theory

Implementing time-dependent BCS pairing dynamics within the Cb-TDHFB framework to capture superfluidity effects in nuclear collisions.

Superheavy Element Synthesis

Studying quasifission dynamics and the determination of fission and quasifission fragment properties relevant to the synthesis of superheavy elements.

Nuclear Structure & Energy Density Functionals

Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of nuclear ground states using Skyrme energy density functionals and finite-range pairing interactions, providing the static foundation for reaction dynamics.

Software

I design and implement high-performance computational codes for nuclear physics, specializing in the large-scale simulation of fission, fusion, and quasifission dynamics using time-dependent density functional theory and relativistic quantum mechanics on 3D grids. More recently, I have turned my attention to the Standard Model and beyond-Standard-Model physics, developing continuum QFT solvers based on functional methods (Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations) and lattice QCD.

muon_fission

Muon-Induced Fission Simulator

Fortran 90 code solving the time-dependent Dirac equation for muonic atoms in fissioning actinide nuclei. Features 4-component spinor evolution on 3D grids with FFT-based Coulomb solver. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA Fortran) versions.

Fortran 90 CUDA Fortran OpenMP FFTW3 Intel MKL LAPACK cuFFT cuBLAS Python HPC

TDHFBCS

Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-BCS

A modern nuclear physics production code for studying low-energy nuclear reactions, including fission, fusion, and quasifission dynamics. Written in Fortran 90 with both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA Fortran) versions.

Fortran 90 CUDA Fortran OpenMP FFTW3 Intel MKL LAPACK cuFFT cuBLAS Python HPC

VU-TDHF3D

Production TDHF-BCS Code

Ongoing maintenance and extension of the production VU-TDHF3D code, a flexible framework for studying nuclear fission, fusion, and quasifission used in published research.

Fortran OpenMP MPI FFTW3 Intel MKL HPC

ChEFT

Chiral Effective Field Theory NN Potentials

Chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon potential calculator computing scattering phase shifts, deuteron binding, SRG-evolved interactions, nuclear matter equation of state, and Skyrme energy density functional parameters via density matrix expansion. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA Fortran) versions.

Fortran 90 CUDA Fortran OpenMP Intel MKL LAPACK cuBLAS cuSOLVER Python HPC

HFB_3D_Cartesian

Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Solver

Static HFB ground-state solver using the canonical-basis formulation with self-consistent pairing correlations. Implements the full Skyrme energy density functional with density-dependent contact pairing on 3D Cartesian grids. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA Fortran) versions.

Fortran 90 CUDA Fortran OpenMP FFTW3 Intel MKL LAPACK cuFFT cuBLAS cuSOLVER Python HPC

HFB_3D_HO

HFB Solver — Harmonic Oscillator Basis

Skyrme HFB solver using a Cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis with Gogny D1S finite-range pairing. Supports constrained HFB (quadrupole/octupole) via augmented Lagrangian method. Complements HFB_3D_Cartesian with a basis-expansion approach for deformed nuclei. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA Fortran) versions.

Fortran 90 CUDA Fortran OpenMP FFTW3 Intel MKL LAPACK cuBLAS cuSOLVER Python HPC

Lattice_QCD

QCD on the Lattice

Lattice QCD simulation code implementing the Wilson gauge action with quenched and dynamical fermion support. Features SU(3) heat bath updates, Wilson fermion propagators with CG/BiCGstab solvers, and Hybrid Monte Carlo for full QCD. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA C++) versions.

C++ CUDA OpenMP cuRAND Python HPC

Functional_QCD

Nonperturbative QCD via DSE/BSE

Continuum QCD solver implementing Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark and ghost propagators, Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson spectroscopy, and Faddeev equations for baryon bound states. Supports Ball-Chiu vertex corrections and multiple gluon interaction models. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA C++) versions.

C++ CUDA OpenMP cuBLAS Python HPC

Electroweak_Numerical

Electroweak DSE Solver

Continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation solver for the full electroweak sector, SU(2)L × U(1)Y. Computes self-consistent fermion and gauge boson propagators with chiral (left/right) dressing functions and evaluates precision observables (S, T, U parameters). Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (CUDA C++) versions.

C++ CUDA OpenMP Python HPC

Minkowski_QED

Minkowski-Space QED Solver

QED solver working directly in Minkowski space using the spectral (Nakanishi) representation for fermion propagators. Implements spectral DSE with vacuum polarization, coupled fermion-photon iterations, and a prototype physics-informed neural network solver. Includes both CPU (OpenMP) and GPU-accelerated (OpenACC) versions.

C++ OpenACC OpenMP Python HPC
